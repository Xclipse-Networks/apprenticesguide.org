Module 01

Machine Construction

The Stator vs. The Rotor

Every DC machine, whether acting as a motor or generator, consists of two primary magnetic circuits. One is stationary (Stator) and one is rotating (Rotor/Armature).

The Stator (Field)

Provides the main magnetic flux. Contains the Yoke, Pole Cores, and Field Windings.

The Rotor (Armature)

The rotating part where EMF is induced. Includes the Commutator and Brushes.

The Commutator Function

The commutator is a mechanical rectifier. In a generator, it converts AC induced in the armature to DC at the terminals. In a motor, it reverses the current direction in each coil as it passes between poles to maintain unidirectional torque.

Module 02

Electromagnetism

Fleming's Rules

Right Hand Rule

Used for **Generators**. Determines the direction of induced current.

Left Hand Rule

Used for **Motors**. Determines the direction of force (motion).

Force on a Conductor

F = BIl

F = Force (N) | B = Flux Density (T)
I = Current (A) | l = Length (m)

Module 03

DC Generators

Types of Excitation

Generators are classified based on how their field windings are energized.

Shunt Wound Field in parallel with armature
Series Wound Field in series with armature
Compound Wound Combination of both Shunt & Series
