Module 02 // Core Equations

Back-EMF Logic

Eb = V - (Ia * Ra)

Eb = Back-EMF (V)
V = Terminal Voltage (V)
Ia = Armature Current (A)
Ra = Armature Resistance (Ω)

Generator Operation

In a generator, the mechanical energy is converted to electrical energy. The generated EMF ($E_g$) must overcome the armature resistance to deliver power to the load.

Eg = V + (Ia * Ra) + V_brush

Motor Dynamics

A motor draws current to produce torque. The back-EMF ($E_b$) opposes the supply voltage and acts as a regulator for the motor's speed and current draw.

Speed (N) ∝ Eb / Φ
