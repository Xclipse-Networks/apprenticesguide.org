Pillar 01 // Physical Architecture

Stator, Rotor & Commutation.

Core Theory

Understanding the physical components that enable energy conversion in DC machines.

  • Yoke: The outer frame providing mechanical support and a return path for magnetic flux.
  • Armature: The rotating part where EMF is induced (Generator) or torque is produced (Motor).
  • Commutator: The mechanical rectifier that converts AC induced in the armature to DC at the brushes.
Pillar 02 // Power Generation

Induced EMF & Field Excitation.

Calculations

The fundamental equation for generated voltage ($E_g$) in a DC generator:

E_g = (Φ × Z × N × P) / (60 × A)
Self-Excitation

Relies on residual magnetism in the field poles to initiate voltage buildup.

Separate Excitation

The field winding is supplied by an external DC source for precise control.

Pillar 03 // Rotational Force

Torque & Back-EMF Logic.

In a DC motor, the armature rotates because of the interaction between the field flux and the armature current.

V = E_b + (I_a × R_a)
!

Back-EMF ($E_b$): Acts as a "governor" for the motor. It is induced in the armature as it rotates through the field, opposing the supply voltage. As speed increases, $E_b$ increases, reducing current flow.

Pillar 04 // Operational Control

Variation & Regulation.

Armature Control

Varying the voltage applied to the armature to control speed below base speed. Constant torque characteristic.

Field Control

Weakening the field flux ($\Phi$) to increase speed above base speed. Constant power characteristic.

Pillar 05 // Troubleshooting

Practical Diagnosis.

Excessive Sparking at Brushes Check MNA / Commutator wear
Motor fails to start (with hum) Check Open Armature / Field Circuit
Overheating under normal load Check Shorted Windings / Ventilation
