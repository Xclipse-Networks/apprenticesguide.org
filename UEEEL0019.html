Module 01 // Armature Reaction

Flux Distortion Effects

Armature reaction occurs when the armature flux distorts and weakens the main field flux ($\Phi$).

  • Shifts the MNA in the direction of rotation (Generators).
  • Shifts the MNA against the direction of rotation (Motors).
  • Causes "Cross-Magnetizing" and "De-Magnetizing" effects.

Countermeasures

Interpoles: Neutralizes reactance voltage
Comp Winding: Cancels distortion in stator

Generator Audit

Eg = V + (Ia * Ra)

Analysis focus: Determining terminal voltage drop under full load due to armature resistance and brush contact drop ($V_b \approx 2V$).

Motor Audit

Eb = V - (Ia * Ra)

Analysis focus: Back-EMF regulation. If $E_b$ drops, $I_a$ surges to maintain torque, increasing thermal stress on the commutator segments.

Critical Diagnostics

Ghost Voltage

Loss of residual magnetism in self-excited shunt generators. Requires "flashing the field."

Commutator Bar Blackening

Often caused by open-circuited armature coils or high-resistance solder joints.

Series Motor Runaway

Critical Risk: Operating a series motor with no load ($I_a \approx 0$) leads to infinite speed potential.
