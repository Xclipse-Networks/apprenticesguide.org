Machine
Principles

The operation of D.C. machines is based on the interaction between magnetic fields and electric currents. Understanding these principles is essential for diagnosing machine faults and performance issues.

The Laws of Induction

1. Faraday’s Law

States that whenever the magnetic flux linking a circuit changes, an EMF is induced. The magnitude of this EMF is proportional to the rate of change of flux linkage.

2. Lenz’s Law

"The direction of an induced current is such that it creates a magnetic field that opposes the change that produced it."

This is why motors produce 'Back-EMF' during rotation.

e = N × (ΔΦ / Δt)

Motor Action (Force)

When a current-carrying conductor is placed in a magnetic field, it experiences a mechanical force.

F = BIl
  • F = Force (Newtons)
  • B = Flux Density (Teslas)
  • I = Current (Amps)
  • l = Length of Conductor (m)

Generator Action (EMF)

When a conductor moves through a magnetic field, voltage is generated across its ends.

e = Blv
  • e = Induced EMF (Volts)
  • B = Flux Density (Teslas)
  • l = Active Length (m)
  • v = Velocity (m/s)

Standard Fleming Hand Rules

Left Hand Rule

Used for MOTORS

Used to find the direction of **Motion** (Force) when the Field and Current directions are known.

Right Hand Rule

Used for GENERATORS

Used to find the direction of induced **Current** when the Field and Motion directions are known.
